Common questions

Frequently asked.

What does an engagement with you typically look like?
+

Most projects start with a 30-minute intro call where we discuss your goals, constraints, and timeline. From there, I scope the work into clear milestones with weekly demos. You get continuous visibility into progress, code reviews on every PR, and predictable shipping. I work async by default and overlap real-time hours when needed.

How do you charge for projects?
+

For most engagements I charge a weekly retainer, which keeps incentives aligned and lets us iterate without time-tracking friction. For tightly-scoped builds I'll quote a fixed fee. Either way, you'll always know what you're paying upfront, and there are no surprise charges.

Do you handle the full stack or just frontend?
+

Full stack. I architect data models, design APIs, write the backend in Node.js or Python, build the frontend in React/Next.js, set up infrastructure, and deploy to production. For mobile, I ship React Native apps to both stores. I prefer end-to-end ownership because it produces better-integrated systems.

Can you join an existing team?
+

Absolutely. I've embedded with engineering teams ranging from 2 to 30 people. I follow your conventions, write code that matches your style, and contribute to PR reviews like a long-term teammate. I won't try to rewrite everything — pragmatism wins.

What's your timezone and availability?
+

I'm based in Surat, India (IST / UTC+5:30) but my schedule is flexible. I regularly overlap with PST, EST, GMT, and APAC clients. Most weeks I keep ~3 hours of guaranteed real-time overlap with whichever timezone the project demands.

Do you take on AI / RAG projects?
+

Yes — AI engineering is one of my main practices. I've shipped clinical RAG systems, agent orchestration platforms, and LLM-powered tools using LangChain, OpenAI, and custom infrastructure. I focus on production-grade pipelines: citations, guardrails, and observability rather than demo-quality magic.

How I work

A deliberate process,
not a checklist.

Software is mostly thinking, sometimes typing. Here's the rhythm I use to take an idea from sketch to production.

PHASE 01

Discover

Deep-dive into the problem space, business goals, users, and existing constraints. I write a one-pager before writing code.

PHASE 02

Architect

Design data models, API surfaces, and system boundaries. Boring tech where possible. Exotic only where justified.

PHASE 03

Build

Ship in tight iterations with continuous review. TypeScript end-to-end, comprehensive tests, automated deploys.

PHASE 04

Iterate

Monitor, measure, and refine. Real users surface real problems — I stay close until the product is stable and growing.
